What I build
Three pillars, one compounding system.

Each works on its own. Together they form the closed loop — context feeds the agents, the agents do the work, the work teaches the system.

01

Company Brain

A central intelligence with read access to where work actually happens, all landing in one common context layer — because agents reason far better when context lives in one place, like a coding agent on a monorepo.

  • One queryable warehouse of company truth
  • Tool registry (MCP) of deterministic actions
  • Skill registry — reusable, DRY & MECE
  • Ask the business questions in plain language
02

Unattended Coding Agent

Specs and tests define success; agents implement until tests pass. PRs are risk-scored — low-risk auto-merges, higher-risk routes to a human. Elevated permissions only inside a sandbox to contain blast radius.

  • Three quality gates: plan, hooks, AGENTS.md
  • Layered review on every PR
  • Model-agnostic harness — swap the best model
  • Auditable merge & deploy trail
03

Agentic Workflows

Domain experts encode their own processes as skills in plain English — no waiting on engineering. The system detects what it can't do and builds the missing capability overnight.

  • Finance, GTM, ops — not just engineering
  • Monitoring agent closes its own gaps
  • Analytics agent runs & ships A/B tests
  • Sprint plans grounded in what shipped
In practice
What a closed loop actually does for you.

Concrete loops I stand up early, because they pay for the engagement on their own.

Self-healing

The overnight builder

A monitoring agent watches every internal query, spots what failed, and builds the missing tool, skill, or view overnight — then merges and deploys. The next morning, the query just works.

Growth

The funnel optimizer

An agent finds the highest-friction step in your funnel, researches best practice, runs an A/B test for a week, picks the winner, and ships it. Then does it again.

Engineering

The sprint planner

With visibility into what actually shipped last cycle and how it met real customer needs, the agent proposes sprint plans that are far more predictable and on-track.

Operations

The encoded expert

Finance and ops teams encode their own workflows in plain English — journal entries, logged rounds, routine processes — without pulling engineers into the loop.

Trust & governance
Autonomy with a hand on the brake.
◆
A policy layer, not a free-for-all

Explicit rules for what agents may do alone, what needs human approval, and what must be logged.

◆
Risk-scored autonomy

Low-risk actions flow; high-stakes ones stop for review. The threshold is yours to set.

◆
Humans where humans matter

High-emotion, high-stakes moments — key sales calls, sensitive issues — stay human-led.

◆
Every action leaves an artifact

So the central intelligence can learn from it — and so you can audit it later.
